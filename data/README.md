# Data Directory
## BSAD 482 – Term Project Milestone 2

This folder contains all datasets used in the analysis of the following decision question:

**Should priority be given to encouraging students toward university programs or skilled trades/apprenticeships given current employment outcomes, earnings potential, and student debt levels?**

---

## Dataset 1: `07-v-avlbal-pro-eng.csv`
**Title:** Average Loan Balance at Time of Leaving School for Full-Time Students in Canada by Gender, Study Level and Institution Type

**Source:** Employment and Social Development Canada – Canada Student Financial Assistance Program

**URL:** https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/0840231b-5bbf-447f-81ce-3ec0673aefc4

**Date Accessed:** March 3, 2026

**Description:** Contains average federal student loan balances (in dollars) at the time students leave school, broken down by gender, study level (certificate/diploma, undergraduate, graduate), and institution type (university, college, private). Data spans academic years 2009-2010 through 2021-2022.

**Usage Restrictions:** Open Government Licence – Canada. Free to use with attribution.

**Used For:** Visualization 1 — trend line chart comparing average loan balances over time for trades/college vs. university students.

---

## Dataset 2: `3710003601-eng.csv`
**Title:** Student Debt from All Sources, by Province of Study and Level of Study

**Source:** Statistics Canada – National Graduates Survey, Table 37-10-0036-01

**URL:** https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/t1/tbl1/en/tv.action?pid=3710003601

**Date Accessed:** March 3, 2026

**Description:** Contains statistics on student debt at graduation including average debt amounts, percentage of graduates who carried large debt loads, and percentage who repaid within three years. Data is broken down by province of study and level of study (college, bachelor's, master's, doctorate) at five-year intervals from 2000 to 2020.

**Usage Restrictions:** Statistics Canada Open Licence. Free to use with attribution.

**Used For:** Visualization 2 — bar chart comparing average debt at graduation across credential types for the most recent reference year (2020).

---

## Dataset 3: `45-n-cal-pt-eng.csv`
**Title:** Number of Canada Apprentice Loan Recipients by Province and Territory

**Source:** Employment and Social Development Canada – Canada Student Financial Assistance Program

**URL:** https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/0840231b-5bbf-447f-81ce-3ec0673aefc4

**Date Accessed:** March 3, 2026

**Description:** Contains the annual number of apprentices who received a Canada Apprentice Loan, broken down by province and territory. Data spans academic years 2014-2015 through 2021-2022. The Canada Apprentice Loan provides interest-free financing to registered apprentices in Red Seal trades during technical training.

**Usage Restrictions:** Open Government Licence – Canada. Free to use with attribution.

**Used For:** Visualization 3 — line chart showing the national trend in apprentice loan uptake over time.

---

## Dataset 4: `3710028001-eng.csv`
**Title:** Characteristics and Median Employment Income of Longitudinal Cohorts of Postsecondary Graduates Two and Five Years After Graduation, by Educational Qualification and Field of Study

**Source:** Statistics Canada – Education and Labour Market Longitudinal Platform (ELMLP), Table 37-10-0280-01

**URL:** https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/t1/tbl1/en/tv.action?pid=3710028001

**Date Accessed:** March 3, 2026

**Description:** Contains median employment income for postsecondary graduates at two and five years after graduation, broken down by educational qualification (college, trades/career, undergraduate, master's, doctorate) and field of study. Income figures are expressed in constant dollars to allow comparison across cohort years.

**Usage Restrictions:** Statistics Canada Open Licence. Free to use with attribution.

**Used For:** Visualization 4 — clustered bar chart comparing median income at 2 and 5 years post-graduation across credential types.
